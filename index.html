PHILIPPINE PLANTS

SIP Group 8 Project

Rafflesia

Description: One of the largest flowers in the world, known for its strong odor.

Care: Rare and parasitic, best observed in the wild.

Where to See: Rainforests of Luzon and Mindanao.

Jade Vine

Description: A rare vine with stunning turquoise flowers.

Care: Requires humid conditions and well-drained soil.

Where to See: Found in tropical forests and botanical gardens.

Waling-waling

Description: Known as the "Queen of Philippine Orchids."

Care: Prefers bright, indirect light and warm temperatures.

Where to See: Found in Mindanao and orchid gardens.

Ylang-ylang

Description: Fragrant yellow flowers used in perfumes.

Care: Grows well in tropical climates with full sun.

Where to See: Common in gardens and plantations.

Swamp Rose Mallow

Description: A pink flowering plant that thrives in wetlands.

Care: Requires moist soil and full sun.

Where to See: Found in swamps and riverbanks.

Arabian Jasmine

Description: The national flower of the Philippines, known for its sweet fragrance.

Care: Prefers warm climates and well-drained soil.

Where to See: Grown in home gardens across the country.

Nepenthes attenboroughii

Description: A rare pitcher plant that traps insects.

Care: Requires high humidity and nutrient-poor soil.

Where to See: Found in the mountains of Palawan.

Hooded Orchid

Description: A unique orchid species with a hood-like structure.

Care: Prefers shaded areas with good air circulation.

Where to See: Found in highland forests.

Monarch Rosemallow

Description: A bright pink hibiscus-like flower.

Care: Thrives in warm and sunny environments.

Where to See: Common in tropical gardens.

Slipper Orchids

Description: Exotic orchids with a pouch-like petal.

Care: Prefers moderate humidity and indirect light.

Where to See: Found in forests and orchid farms.

Asters

Description: Small daisy-like flowers in various colors.

Care: Needs full sun and regular watering.

Where to See: Grown in gardens and floral farms.

Corpse Flower

Description: A rare flower known for its foul odor.

Care: Can only be observed in conservation areas.

Where to See: Found in botanical gardens and rainforests.

Water Lilies

Description: Floating aquatic flowers with broad leaves.

Care: Thrives in still or slow-moving water.

Where to See: Found in ponds and lakes.
